Internships with General Dynamics

Bluefin Robotics

Bluefin designs, develops, and manufactures autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) for Defense, commercial, and scientific customers. Working within teams of mechanical, electrical, systems, and manufacturing engineers, the marine operations group is primarily responsible for the field testing of all projects, assessing the vehicles' performance and ability aptly carry out the functions for which it has been designed.

Over the summers of 2016 and 2017, I was trained as an AUV operator on a variety of specialized AUVs. In my second summer (2017) I was primarily assigned to the Knifefish UUV project (right). Knifefish is a heavyweight (21" hull) mine countermeasure vehicle designed to increase the mine hunting capabilities of the US Navy. The project passed contractor trials in October 2017.

Specific Responsibilities

  • Mission Planning
  • Dive Preparation, Systems checks (Electrical, Mechanical, Communications)
  • Vehicle operation and monitoring
  • Data analysis (Sonar imaging, Dynamics, Control, tracking, etc.)
  • Troubleshooting (electrial, mechanical, communications systems)
  • Communication System Configuration
